01 -

2019

MANEKI NEKO

Challenge

Design a children's sustainable fashion shop interior for adults. Produce and install all fixtures and shop refurbishment in 10 weeks.

The shop is located in an old Dutch house groundfloor. My concept was for it to look rich, serious and playful. Hence, taking advantage of the long and narrow space, I divided the interior into "play/fixed display/flexible display" areas.

I achieved flexible displaying by designing modular racks and shelves fixtures that can be easily assembled and dissembled by screwing them on the fixed fixture rack. I also designed racks with cotton string and a longitudinal bar crossing an ackward space of two columns to create undistinguished corridors for dynamism in the circulation of the shop. For horizontal display I designed floating wooden shelve, so the retailer can switch stock just by herself.

2017

HEADREST TO TRAIN CEPHALIC CONTROL IN PEOPLE WITH NEUROLOGICAL DISEASES

Challenge

There is no brief! After user insight (interviews, customer journey maps and persona) I detected a need and gave it a solution: why not reduce progression pace of loss in cephalic muscle control?

My headrest's objective is to prolongate their ability to control their head posture to make it stand up. It is unique in the current market.

The design is fairly simple so MS associations, normally limited on their budget, can build one. It has an integrated alarm for the feedback during training, to allow the autonomy.

2017

AVOCADO LOUNGE HAMMOCK

Challenge

Houses getting smaller and smaller, can we offer a secondary furniture to the main essential home furniture?

Combining a storage space used for chilling we create a reason to spend such use of space.

The hammock is a piece for outdoors. However, prototyping a metalic structure and seweing it our result is a chilling experience for indoors: nowadays not many people dispose of a garden or balcony. What do people do in a Hammock? Sleep or use small entertainment objects such as books or phones. Therefore, to supply the storage space we integrated a bookcase to have always entertainment in a handy place.

Ricardo Garre, Celia Soler, Camila Bresciani, Sheila Segarra

05 -

2015

OLYMPIC PODIUM IN BRASIL

Challenge

To efficiently transport & assemble the product to a non-constructive terrain with little infrastructure.

Reinventing the experience of going up the podium, my team and I did a new series of steps for the athlete to get to his place by stepping on his opponents. The red cross is the sponsor's logo, it is actually an autonomous LED light.

The podium's internal structure are aluminium cantilevers with ramification to increase inertia. For balance, it is screwed to steel plates buried in the ground. PVC panels with PU antislip finish are rivited to the aluminium structure. Hence, we not only accomplish a secure demountable transportation but create an impactful design for winners.

Paula Usó, María Bárcena, Marc Monferrer
